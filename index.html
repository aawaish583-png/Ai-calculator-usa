What is AdSense Page RPM?

Page Revenue Per Mille (RPM) is a crucial metric that represents your estimated earnings for every 1,000 page views you receive. It is not exactly what you have earned; rather, it's a calculation based on your current performance to help you understand the value of your traffic.

The formula for RPM is: $RPM = (Estimated Earnings / Number of Page Views) * 1000$

How to Increase Your AdSense RPM?

  • Target High CPC Keywords: Focus on niches like Finance, Insurance, and Tech.
  • Improve Ad Placement: Use "Above the Fold" placements for better visibility.
  • User Experience: A faster site leads to more page views and better engagement.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is RPM the same as CPM?

No. CPM (Cost Per Mille) is what advertisers pay for 1,000 impressions, while RPM is what you, the publisher, earn per 1,000 views after Google's cut.

2. What is a "good" AdSense RPM?

It depends on your niche. For entertainment, $2-$5 is common. For finance or legal niches, it can go up to $50+.

3. Why is my RPM decreasing?

Seasonal changes, poor traffic quality, or low-value keywords can cause a drop in RPM.
