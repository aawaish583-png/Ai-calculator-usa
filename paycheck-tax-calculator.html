Understanding Your 2026 Tax Breakdown

Federal Income Tax System

The United States uses a progressive federal tax system with seven brackets for 2026 (projected). Each portion of your income is taxed at increasing rates:

  • 10% on first $11,600 of taxable income
  • 12% on income from $11,601 to $47,150
  • 22% on income from $47,151 to $100,525
  • 24% on income from $100,526 to $191,950
  • 32% on income from $191,951 to $243,725
  • 35% on income from $243,726 to $609,350
  • 37% on income over $609,350

State Tax Variations

State taxes create significant differences in take-home pay across the USA. While states like Texas and Florida have no state income tax, California and New York have some of the highest rates. Some states use flat rates (Colorado 4.75%, Illinois 5%), while others use progressive systems similar to federal tax.

FICA Taxes (Social Security & Medicare)

These mandatory payroll taxes fund specific programs: Social Security (retirement/disability) and Medicare (healthcare for seniors). The combined rate is 7.65% for employees, with employers matching this amount. High earners pay an additional 0.9% Medicare tax on income over $200,000.

Example 3: Senior Professional ($150,000 in New York)

A high earner in New York making $150,000 would pay about $26,600 in federal taxes, $10,275 in state taxes, and $10,613 in FICA taxes (including Additional Medicare Tax). Their take-home pay would be roughly $102,512 annually, or $8,543 per month.

A married couple filing jointly with $250,000 income in Illinois would pay approximately $44,000 federal, $12,500 state (5% flat), and $19,125 FICA. Their combined take-home: $174,375 annually, or $14,531 monthly.
