10 Essential Tips for Choosing the Perfect Business Name

1. Keep it Short & Memorable

1-3 syllables ideal. Easy to remember, spell, and share verbally.

2. Check Domain Availability

Secure .com first, then consider .io, .co, or other relevant TLDs.

3. Consider Global Appeal

Ensure it doesn't mean something negative in other languages.

4. Test with Your Audience

Get feedback from potential customers before finalizing.

5. Think Long-Term

Avoid names that limit future growth or product expansion.

6. Check Trademark Availability

Search USPTO database to avoid legal issues.

7. Say it Aloud

Ensure it's easy to pronounce and sounds good when spoken.

8. Consider SEO

Include relevant keywords if possible for better search visibility.

9. Visualize the Logo

Consider how the name will work visually in logos and branding.

10. Sleep on It

Give yourself time - don't rush this critical decision.

The Name Testing Checklist

Before finalizing any name, run through this checklist:

  • Is it easy to spell and pronounce?
  • Is the .com domain available (or reasonable alternative)?
  • Are social media handles available?
  • Does it sound good when said aloud?
  • Is it memorable after hearing it once?
  • Does it scale with business growth?
  • Is it trademark available in your industry?
  • Does it work internationally?
  • Does it reflect your brand values?
  • Do you still like it after a week?

Frequently Asked Questions: Business Names 2026

How many business names should I test before choosing? ‚ñº

Start with 10-20 options, narrow to 3-5 finalists, then test those with your target audience. Our generator creates 20+ names per search to give you plenty of options. Consider factors like domain availability, trademark clearance, and brand fit for each.

Is .com still necessary in 2026? ‚ñº

.com is still preferred for credibility, especially for B2B and traditional businesses. However, .io (tech), .ai (AI companies), .co (startups), and industry-specific TLDs like .tech or .store are increasingly accepted. Always secure the .com if possible, even if you use another TLD.

Should I include keywords in my business name? ‚ñº

For SEO benefits, yes - but balance with brandability. "Austin Plumbing" gets local SEO benefits but is generic. "PipePerfect Plumbing" is more brandable. Consider: Exact match keywords help SEO but may be less memorable. Brandable names with keywords work best.

How do I check if a name is trademarked? ‚ñº

Search the USPTO database (US Patent and Trademark Office) for exact matches and similar names in your industry. Also check state business registries and do a general Google search. Consider hiring a trademark attorney for important names to ensure thorough clearance.

Can I change my business name later? ‚ñº

Yes, but it's expensive and confusing. Rebranding costs include: new domain, logo redesign, marketing materials, legal filings, lost SEO, and customer confusion. It's better to spend time getting the name right initially. Some companies successfully rebrand, but plan for 3-6 months of transition.

What makes a name "brandable"? ‚ñº

Brandable names are: memorable, distinctive, emotionally appealing, and suggest positive attributes. They often work well as verbs ("Google it"), scale beyond initial offerings (Amazon started with books), and create strong visual associations (Apple's logo).

Should I use my own name for the business? ‚ñº

Personal names work for personal brands, consulting, or creative services. Disadvantages include: difficult to sell, may limit growth beyond you, and can be hard to trademark. Consider: "Johnson Consulting" works, but "Johnson Technologies" might limit future saleability.

How important are social media handles? ‚ñº

Very important for marketing consistency. Check availability on Twitter, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, and TikTok before finalizing. Variations with underscores or extra words reduce brand recognition. If exact handles aren't available, consider slight modifications that still maintain brand identity.

What's the ideal length for a business name? ‚ñº

2-3 words maximum, 10-15 characters ideal. Short names (1-2 syllables) are most memorable. According to studies, the average business name length for successful startups is 10.8 characters. Avoid names over 20 characters as they're hard to remember and type.

How accurate is the domain availability check? ‚ñº

Our tool checks against common domain registrars and provides availability indicators. However, always verify directly with domain registrars like GoDaddy, Namecheap, or Google Domains before purchasing. Domain status can change rapidly, so act quickly on available names you like.
