What is RPM on YouTube? (2026 Edition)

RPM stands for Revenue Per Mille (or Revenue Per Thousand). It's the amount of money you earn for every 1,000 views on your YouTube videos. Unlike CPM (Cost Per Mille), which measures what advertisers pay, RPM tells you what you actually take home after YouTube's 45% cut.

üí∞ RPM Formula

Earnings = (Views √∑ 1,000) √ó RPM

Example: 100,000 views at $3.50 RPM = (100,000 √∑ 1,000) √ó 3.50 = $350

RPM vs CPM: What's the Difference?

Many creators confuse RPM and CPM, but understanding the difference is crucial for accurate earnings estimation:

Metric What It Measures YouTube's Cut Who Uses It
RPM Your actual earnings per 1,000 views Already deducted (45%) Creators (You)
CPM What advertisers pay per 1,000 impressions Not yet deducted Advertisers & YouTube

Key Insight: Your RPM is typically 55% of your CPM (because YouTube keeps 45%). A $10 CPM usually results in a $5.50 RPM for the creator.

Frequently Asked Questions: YouTube RPM 2026

What is a good RPM on YouTube in 2026? ‚ñº

A good RPM ranges from $3-8 for most channels. Gaming channels typically see $1.5-3 RPM, while finance channels can achieve $5-15 RPM. The average across all niches is approximately $3.50 RPM.

How much does YouTube pay for 1 million views? ‚ñº

1 million views typically earns $3,500 at $3.50 RPM. However, earnings range from $1,500 (gaming at $1.50 RPM) to $15,000 (finance at $15 RPM). Geography also plays a huge role - 1M US views pays much more than 1M Indian views.

Why is my RPM so low? ‚ñº

Low RPM can be caused by: 1) Audience from low-paying countries, 2) Gaming or entertainment niche, 3) Short videos with few ads, 4) Low watch time, 5) High use of ad blockers by your audience, or 6) Not enabling all ad formats.

How can I check my actual RPM? ‚ñº

In YouTube Studio: Analytics ‚Üí Revenue ‚Üí RPM. You can view overall RPM, RPM by video, and RPM by country. This shows your actual earnings per 1000 views after YouTube's 45% cut.

Does RPM include Super Chats and memberships? ‚ñº

No. RPM only measures ad revenue. YouTube reports RPM separately from Super Chat, channel membership, and merchandise revenue. Our calculator focuses on ad revenue RPM only.

How much does YouTube take from my earnings? ‚ñº

YouTube takes 45% of ad revenue. If an advertiser pays $10 CPM, YouTube keeps $4.50 and you receive $5.50 RPM. This 45/55 split is standard across the YouTube Partner Program.

Which countries have the highest RPM? ‚ñº

United States has the highest RPM ($4-12), followed by Canada, UK, Australia, Germany, and Switzerland. Tier 2 countries include most of Western Europe. Tier 3 includes Asian, African, and South American countries.

Do shorts have different RPM? ‚ñº

Yes, Shorts RPM is typically 10-30% of long-form RPM. The YouTube Shorts Fund and ad revenue sharing for Shorts pay significantly less per view than regular videos. Focus on long-form for maximum earnings.

When do RPM rates increase? ‚ñº

RPM increases during holiday seasons (Q4), tax season (finance channels), back-to-school (education), and when advertiser competition is high. RPM typically peaks in November-December each year.

How accurate is this RPM calculator? ‚ñº

Our calculator uses 2026 projections based on current trends, historical data, and creator reports. Actual earnings vary based on your specific content, audience, and market conditions. Use it as an estimate, not a guarantee.
